What Paint Correction Fixes

Every vehicle picks up swirl marks, water spots, and light scratches over time — usually from automatic car washes, poor wash technique, or years of environmental exposure. Paint correction uses a machine polisher and graduated compounds to level the clear coat and remove those defects, restoring the true depth and gloss of your paint.

We offer both 1-step correction (for light swirls and scratches) and 2-step correction (for deeper defects and oxidation), and we'll always assess your paint's condition before recommending which one you actually need — we're not going to upsell you into a 2-step job a 1-step will handle just fine.

Common question

Will paint correction remove all scratches on my car?
Paint correction removes the vast majority of surface defects — swirl marks, water spots, light scratches, and oxidation. Scratches that have cut through the clear coat down to the base coat or primer can't be fully corrected through polishing alone and may need touch-up paint. We'll always give you an honest assessment before we start so you know exactly what to expect.
